The Marvel Cinematic Universe Scores 7 2016 MTV Movie Award Nominations

"Avengers: Age of Ultron" quickly became the biggest adventure the Marvel Cinematic Universe had ever seen, which is exactly why it won the nomination for Movie of the Year! Earth's Mightiest Heroes assembled like never before in the film, leading to a nomination for Best Ensemble Cast. The Avengers united to take down the mighty Ultron (James Spader), who not only scored a nomination for Best Villain but also Best Virtual Performance!

"Avengers: Age of Ultron" also featured one of the most brutal bouts seen on the big screen when Iron Man (Robert Downey Jr.) went head-to-head with Hulk (Mark Ruffalo), grabbing a well-deserved nomination for Best Fight. Meanwhile, Captain America will square off against Ant-Man as stars Chris Evans and Paul Rudd with the two competing to become Best Hero for their performances in "Avengers: Age of Ultron" and "Ant-Man"! Make sure to cast your votes as often as once a day through Sunday, March 20 and tune in to the ceremony Sunday, April 10 at 8:00 PM ET on MTV! Stay tuned to Marvel.com for all the latest news and updates on the Marvel Cinematic Universe.

MARVEL'S AGENTS OF S.H.I.E.L.D.' COMING TO WONDERCON

The "Marvel’s Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D." panel will be moderated by Marvel’s Head of Television and series Executive Producer, Jeph Loeb, and will include a Q&A discussion where fans can get a glimpse of what’s ahead for the team in Season 3. Fans lucky enough to attend will also get a sneak peek at an upcoming episode of the series before it airs on ABC as well as a limited edition show-themed mini-poster, featuring never-before-seen art by Marvel comic artist, Mike McKone. Can’t make the panel? Fans can catch the cast at a poster signing after the panel in the private signing room.

Chadwick Boseman Introduces the Black Panther in New Interview

TIt’s safe to say that the introduction of the Black Panther to Marvel’s “Captain America: Civil War” brought a shout of joy and triumph to lips of many a comic book fan, as did the announcement of actor Chadwick Boseman in the role. For Boseman, it’s an immersion in a brand-new world, for which he appears more than up to the task.

“You meet me as the Prince of Wakanda,” he says of his character’s debut in the film. “You meet me as a politician/monarch, not as a super hero. [But,] I am already a Black Panther.”Boseman’s entry into the established Marvel Cinematic Universe came as something of a surprise to him, albeit a pleasant one.Boseman’s known for playing real-life historical figures such as James Brown and Jackie Robinson, but cites his personal favorite thing about portraying the Black Panther.
